## What is Cor?

Cor puts **Obi** in your product. It learns each customer's goals, builds a personalized plan, and sees their screen to guide them and get things done.

These are the developer docs — how to add Obi to your app, tell it about your customers, and pull the data back out.

Cor has three building blocks:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Obi SDK" icon="code" href="/sdk/installation">
    Embed Obi in your app with one snippet, and tell it who the customer is.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Session Reporting API" icon="chart-line" href="/api-reference/introduction">
    Pull session data, summaries, and full transcripts for every customer.
  </Card>
  <Card title="User Context API" icon="user" href="/api-reference/introduction">
    Push what you know about a customer so Obi tailors its guidance.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Obi does

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Understands goals" icon="bullseye">
    Learns what each customer is trying to do and tailors the guidance to them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Guides on their screen" icon="arrow-pointer">
    Sees the page the customer is on and walks them through it, step by step.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Gets things done" icon="bolt">
    Executes actions when needed — not just telling customers what to click.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reports back" icon="chart-line">
    Every session produces a summary and transcript you can pull through the API.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How the pieces fit together

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the SDK">
    Add Obi to your app. The widget loads on every page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify the customer">
    Tell Obi who the signed-in user is. This powers personalization, targeting, and reporting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Obi guides and acts">
    Obi runs personalized sessions — guiding the customer and getting things done.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report and enrich">
    Pull transcripts and summaries through the API, and push extra context back to Obi.
  </Step>
</Steps>
